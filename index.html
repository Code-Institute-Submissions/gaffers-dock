Our Services

  • We offer advice and support on anything concerning your boat's rig. Wether it's just a small adjustment, an alteration or a completely new design.
  • If you want to know, for instance, how to parcel and serve wire, splice a Liverpool eyesplice or make baggywrinkles, we offer tutored lessons regularly.
  • We provide full service on all standing and running rigging parts, as well as on spars and fittings.
  • In our workshop we have the possibility to make masts and spars in good quality Spruce or Oregon Pine. Construct bronze, galvanized o stainless steel fittings, as well as do all rope and wire works necessary.

Current Projects
⎯ ✵ ⎯

"Nellie"

The owner and Master Rigger Janet McGaff is making a new shroud for "Nellie". The 42ft Smack Yawl is getting all its' standing rigging replaced. The wire for the shrouds is first wormed with tarred hemp yarn, then parceled with jute fabric and served tightly with hemp spunyarn. Finally, we add a protective coating of Stockholm tar and linseed oil. With regular care the wire will serve "Nellie" for many years to come.

"Lazy Flax"

"Lazy Flax" is a 28ft Heard Pilot Cutter. Her original deadeyes from 1908 was in no shape for further adventures on the Big Blue. The new deadeyes we manufactured for her was made out of ash. To make them withstand the ruff conditions of the North Sea, they are thoroughly cooked in linseed oil. The eyesplice in the background was made five years ago by our Senior Rigger Hugh Macluff, and still looks as good as new.

"Wilhelmina"

We have made countless Liverpool eyesplices over the years. Here, Janet is making one for the 1922 36ft Bawley "Wilhelmina". With the thimble tightly clamped in the vice and the marlin spike set firmly in the wire, Janet smoothly gets each tuck in place. She will make six eyesplices to the stays in "Wilhelmina's" new rig. In addition to the stays, we will construct a new gaff boom and a 15ft long bowsprit out of Spruce.

About Us
⎯ ✵ ⎯

Gaffer’s Dock is one of the few remaining riggers in the world that specialise in traditional gaff rigged boats. In 1919, with 30 years of experience of working and owning gaff rigged boats, Duncan McGaff started up Gaffer’s Dock in a shed at the Docks here in Broadford on Isle of Skye.
Gaffer’ Dock is still a family owned company, and the Master Rigger Janet McGaff is the fourth generation McGaff’s that has the skills and craftsmanship to make and maintain traditional gaff yacht rigs. Janet McGaff runs the company together with her husband Greg and the Dock’s cat “Baggywrinkle”.
