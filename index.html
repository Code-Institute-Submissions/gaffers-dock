Current Projects

"Nellie"

The owner and Master Rigger Janet McGaff is making a new shroud for "Nellie". The 42ft Smack Yawl is getting all it's standing rigging replaced. The wire for the shrouds is first wormed with tarred hemp yarn, then parceled with jute fabric and served tightly with hemp spunyarn. Finally it gets a protective coating of a mixture of Stockhom tar and linseed oil. With regular care "Nellie's" rig wil serve her well for many years to come.

"Lazy Flax"

"Lazy Flax" is a 28ft Heard Pilot Cutter. Her original deadeyes from 1908 was in no shape for further adventures on the Big Blue. The new deadeyes we manufactured for her is made out of ash. To make them withstand the, sometimes harsh, atlantic weather they are thoroughly cooked in linseed oil. The eyesplice in the background was made five years ago by our Senior Rigger Hugh Macluff, and still looks as good as new.

"Wilhelmina"

We have made countless Liverpool eyesplices over the years. Here, Janet is making one for the 1922 36ft Bawley "Wilhelmina". With the thimble tightly clamped in the vice and the marlin spike set firmly in the wire, Janet smothly gets the tucks in splice. She will make six eyesplices to the stays in "Wilhelmina's" new rig. In addition to the stays, we will construct a new gaff boom out of Spruce, as well as a 15ft long bowsprit of Oregon Pine.
